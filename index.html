Why Most People Fail to Stop Habits — And What Actually Works

Most habit-breaking advice targets the behavior itself. That's why it rarely works. Eric Edmeades has spent two decades discovering that it's not what you stop that matters — it's how your nervous system is wired to stop. That's your Stop Style™.

There are four distinct Stop Styles. If you've ever tried willpower, cold-turkey, or "just deciding" to quit something and failed, there's a very specific reason: you were using the wrong style for your neurology.

Using the wrong three styles doesn't just fail — it reinforces the habit and chips away at your self-esteem with every attempt.

In this free class, Eric walks you through the science of Behavior Change Dynamics™ — including evolutionary mismatch, shame release, and the role of identity in habit cessation — and helps you identify exactly which Stop Style is yours.

This isn't a motivational talk. It's a precision framework built on 15+ years of working with real people, rooted in evolutionary biology and behavioral psychology.

Once you know your Stop Style, change becomes effortless — not because you got stronger, but because you stopped fighting yourself.

Eric Edmeades is one of the world's leading authorities on behavioral change. For over 20 years, he has focused on a single core question: why we do what we do — and how to stop doing the things we most want to stop.
